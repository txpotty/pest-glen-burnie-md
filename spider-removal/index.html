Recognizing the Signs You Need Professional Spider Control

While seeing an occasional spider indoors is common, certain signs can indicate a more significant infestation that warrants professional attention. Recognizing these signs early can help you address the problem before it escalates.

Increased Spider Sightings

One of the most obvious indicators of a potential spider problem is a noticeable increase in the number of spiders you are seeing inside your home or business. If you are encountering spiders more frequently than usual, or in multiple areas of your property, it suggests that there is a breeding population present. This could mean spiders are reproducing within the structure or that conditions are favorable for them to enter and thrive.

Presence of Extensive Webbing

Spiders are known for their webs, and while a few individual webs might not be cause for alarm, extensive or widespread webbing throughout your property can be a strong sign of a significant infestation. Pay attention to areas like corners of rooms, ceilings, around windows and doors, basements, attics, and crawl spaces. Heavy webbing indicates that numerous spiders are actively building webs for catching prey or for shelter.

Discovery of Egg Sacs

Finding spider egg sacs is a clear indication that spiders are reproducing on your property. These sacs, which vary in appearance depending on the spider species, contain dozens to hundreds of spiderlings. Discovering egg sacs means a new generation of spiders is about to hatch, potentially leading to a rapid increase in the spider population. Prompt removal of egg sacs is crucial for controlling an infestation.

Seeing Specific Spider Species

While many spiders are harmless, the presence of certain species, particularly those known to be venomous like black widows or brown recluse spiders, is a serious concern and requires immediate professional intervention. These spiders pose a health risk, and attempts to remove them without proper knowledge and equipment can be dangerous. If you suspect you have venomous spiders, it is essential to contact pest control professionals immediately.

Other Pest Issues

An increase in spiders can sometimes be a symptom of other underlying pest issues. Spiders feed on other insects, so a surge in their numbers might indicate that you have an abundance of their prey – other pests like ants, cockroaches, or flies. Addressing these other pest issues is often necessary to effectively control the spider population in the long term.

Spider Bites

Experiencing unexplained spider bites is a significant indicator that spiders are present and active within your living or working spaces. While many spider bites are not serious, reactions can vary depending on the individual and the spider species. If you, your family, or your employees are experiencing bites, it's a strong signal that a professional inspection and treatment are needed to identify the source and eliminate the risk.

Persistent Spider Problems

If you've attempted DIY methods for spider control without success, and the problem persists or worsens, it's time to call in the experts. Professional pest control services have the knowledge, experience, and specialized products and equipment to effectively deal with stubborn spider infestations. We can identify why your previous attempts were unsuccessful and implement a more targeted and powerful solution.

Visible Spiders Around Windows and Doors

Seeing spiders frequently around windows and doors suggests they are using these as entry points into your property. This indicates a need for identifying and sealing these gaps, along with exterior treatments to deter spiders from approaching the structure. Prompt action can prevent a larger infestation from developing indoors.

Hearing Scratching or Skittering Sounds

While not directly related to spiders, hearing unexplained scratching or skittering sounds within walls, attics, or crawl spaces could indicate the presence of larger pests like rodents or even wildlife. These pests can also attract spiders as a food source or create conditions favorable for spiders to inhabit. A comprehensive pest inspection is often beneficial in such cases to identify all contributing factors.

Unpleasant Odors

In rare cases of extremely large infestations or certain spider species, an unpleasant odor might be noticeable. This is not common but can be another indicator of a significant pest problem, including potentially spiders, that requires professional assessment. It's always best to investigate unusual odors promptly.

Understanding Common Spiders in Glen Burnie, MD

Glen Burnie, Maryland, like many areas, is home to various spider species. While the thought of spiders might be unsettling, most common types found in residential and commercial settings in our region are not considered dangerous and are primarily a nuisance. However, knowing the types of spiders you might encounter can alleviate some anxiety and help you understand the importance of professional identification and removal.

Common House Spiders

The most frequently encountered spiders indoors are often collectively referred to as 'house spiders'. These are typically small to medium-sized spiders that build irregular webs, often in corners, under furniture, and in undisturbed areas. Their bites are rare and usually produce only mild, localized reactions. While not harmful, a large population of house spiders can create a messy environment with abundant webs. Examples include the American house spider (Achaearanea tepidariorum) and the cellar spider (Pholcus phalangioides), also known as daddy longlegs.

Cellar Spiders (Daddy Longlegs)

Cellar spiders are instantly recognizable by their exceptionally long, thin legs and small bodies. They are commonly found in basements, attics, and other damp, dark areas. They build large, messy webs. Despite their fearsome appearance to some, cellar spiders are harmless to humans. Their bite is not medically significant, and they are often beneficial as they prey on other insects and even other spiders. While not a threat, their extensive webbing can be unsightly and necessitates removal for cleanliness.

Orb-Weaver Spiders

Orb-weaver spiders are known for constructing the classic, intricate wagon-wheel-shaped webs often seen outdoors between trees, shrubs, or structures. These spiders are beneficial predators of flying insects and play an important role in the ecosystem. While they can occasionally be found near entry points to homes, they rarely venture indoors in significant numbers. Their bites are uncommon unless threatened and are typically not dangerous. Species like the Garden Orb-Weaver (Argiope aurantia) are frequently seen in gardens during the warmer months.

Jumping Spiders

Jumping spiders are small, compact spiders characterized by their excellent vision and jerky, quick movements. Unlike many spiders that rely on webs to catch prey, jumping spiders actively stalk and pounce on their victims. They do not build traditional webs, although they may create small silken retreats for resting or egg-laying. Jumping spiders are generally considered harmless and are actually quite fascinating to observe. They are more likely to be seen outdoors but can occasionally find their way indoors. Their bites are rare and minor.

Wolf Spiders

Wolf spiders are large, hairy, ground-dwelling spiders that do not build webs to catch prey. Instead, they are active hunters, using their speed and agility to catch insects and other invertebrates. They are often seen running across floors or pavements, particularly at night. Wolf spiders may enter homes seeking shelter or prey. While their size can be intimidating, their bites are usually not dangerous, although they can be painful. They are not aggressive unless provoked.

Sac Spiders

Yellow sac spiders are small, pale spiders that build silken sacs for resting and egg-laying, rather than elaborate webs. They are often found in corners of rooms or near ceilings. Sac spiders are known for their potential to bite, particularly if accidentally trapped against the skin. While the bite can be painful and may cause localized swelling or redness, it is generally not considered medically significant for most individuals.

Potentially Venomous Spiders in Maryland

While less common than the harmless species, it's important to be aware of the potential for encountering medically significant spiders in Maryland. The two primary concerns are the Black Widow and the Brown Recluse. Black Widows are identified by the iconic red hourglass marking on their abdomen and are typically found in dark, undisturbed areas like basements, sheds, and woodpiles. Brown Recluse spiders are less common in most parts of Maryland but can be found. They are identified by a violin-shaped mark on their cephalothorax. Bites from these spiders require medical attention. Professional identification and removal are critical if you suspect the presence of either of these spiders.

Preventative Measures to Reduce Spider Activity

While professional spider removal in Glen Burnie, MD is highly effective at eliminating existing infestations, implementing preventative measures is key to minimizing the risk of future spider problems. These simple steps can make your property less attractive to spiders and help keep them out.

Seal Entry Points

Spiders, like many other pests, enter homes and buildings through cracks, gaps, and openings. Inspect your foundation, walls, windows, and doors for any visible gaps or cracks. Use caulk or sealant to seal these openings. Repair damaged screens on windows and doors, and ensure that door sweeps are in good condition and seal tightly against the threshold. Pay attention to areas where pipes and wires enter the building, ensuring they are properly sealed. Addressing these entry points is one of the most effective ways to prevent spiders from getting inside.

Reduce Clutter

Spiders often seek shelter in cluttered and undisturbed areas. Reducing clutter both inside and outside your home can significantly decrease potential hiding spots for spiders. Inside, organize storage areas like basements, attics, and garages. Use sealed plastic containers to store items rather than cardboard boxes. Outside, remove piles of debris, firewood stacked directly against the house, and overgrown vegetation. Keep storage sheds and outbuildings tidy.

Manage Moisture

Many spider species are attracted to damp and humid environments. Repair any leaky pipes or faucets, both indoors and outdoors. Ensure good ventilation in basements and crawl spaces, perhaps using dehumidifiers if necessary. Address any drainage issues around your property to prevent standing water. Reducing moisture levels makes your home less hospitable to spiders and the insects they prey on.

Clean Regularly

Regular cleaning can help to disrupt spider activity and remove potential food sources. Vacuum and sweep frequently to remove spiders, webs, and egg sacs. Pay particular attention to corners, baseboards, and under furniture. Dust surfaces regularly to remove webs. Dispose of trash properly and in sealed containers to avoid attracting other insects that spiders feed on.

Trim Vegetation

Overgrown vegetation touching the sides of your house can provide a bridge for spiders to access your exterior walls and potential entry points. Trim bushes, trees, and shrubs so that they are not touching the structure. Keep grass mowed and remove excessive mulch or ground cover directly adjacent to the foundation. Creating a barrier of gravel or rock around the foundation can also deter spiders.

Reduce Exterior Lighting

Exterior lights, especially bright ones, can attract flying insects, which are a primary food source for many spiders. This, in turn, can attract spiders to areas around your home. Consider using yellow or sodium vapor bulbs, which are less attractive to insects, or use motion-sensor lighting so lights are only on when needed. Position lights so that they don't shine directly on entryways.

Inspect Items Brought Indoors

Before bringing items like boxes, plants, or firewood indoors, inspect them for spiders or egg sacs. Spiders can easily hitch a ride into your home on these items. A quick visual check can prevent introducing unwanted guests.

A proactive approach is often the most cost-effective and stress-free way to keep your property spider-free.
