Our Comprehensive Squirrel Removal and Prevention Process

Successfully addressing a squirrel infestation involves more than just removing the visible pests. It requires a systematic approach to identify the source, eliminate the problem, and prevent future occurrences. Our comprehensive squirrel removal process is designed for maximum effectiveness and long-term peace of mind.

Initial Inspection and Assessment

The first crucial step is a thorough inspection of your property. Our experienced technicians will carefully examine both the interior and exterior of your home or business to identify signs of squirrel activity, potential entry points, and the extent of the infestation. We assess areas like the roofline, eaves, chimneys, vents, attics, basements, and crawl spaces. This detailed assessment allows us to develop a tailored removal strategy specific to your situation.

Identifying Entry Points

Squirrels are incredibly agile and can squeeze through surprisingly small openings. Our inspection focuses on locating these potential entry points, which can include damaged soffits, fascia boards, roof vents, uncapped chimneys, gaps in siding, or even chewed holes around windows and doors. Precisely identifying these access points is vital for both removal and exclusion.

Assessing the Infestation Level

Understanding how many squirrels are present and if there are any nesting activities (especially with young) helps us determine the most appropriate and humane removal methods. We look for evidence like nesting materials, droppings, and chewed debris.

Safe and Humane Squirrel Removal

Once the infestation is assessed, we employ safe and humane methods to remove the squirrels from your property. We prioritize the well-being of the animals while ensuring their effective relocation away from your home.

Trapping and Relocation

Live trapping is a common and humane method for squirrel removal. We strategically place traps in areas of high squirrel activity. Once a squirrel is caught, we humanely transport it to a suitable location far from your property, allowing it to thrive in a natural environment. Our methods comply with all local regulations regarding wildlife trapping and relocation.

One-Way Doors

In some cases, we utilize one-way door systems. These devices are installed over the main entry point and allow squirrels to leave the structure but prevent them from re-entering. This is particularly effective when dealing with a single entry point and helps ensure all squirrels, including any juveniles that might be present, can exit safely.

Exclusion and Damage Repair

Removing the squirrels is only half the battle. Preventing their return is essential. Our exclusion services involve sealing all identified entry points to make your property impenetrable to squirrels and other nuisance wildlife. We use durable, squirrel-resistant materials to repair any damage caused by the infestation.

Sealing Entry Points

We use sturdy materials like heavy gauge wire mesh, metal flashing, and durable sealants to permanently close off all potential entry points. This includes reinforcing vulnerable areas that squirrels might target in the future.

Repairing Damage

Recognizing the Signs: Do You Need Squirrel Removal in Glen Burnie?

Left unchecked, a squirrel infestation can lead to substantial problems. Identifying the signs early allows for prompt intervention and minimizes potential damage and health risks. If you're a homeowner or business owner in Glen Burnie, be vigilant for the following indicators that may necessitate professional squirrel removal services:

Sounds in Walls or Attic

One of the most common signs of squirrels indoors is the presence of unusual noises. You might hear scratching, scurrying, gnawing, or chattering sounds coming from your walls, ceiling, or attic, particularly during the daytime. Squirrels are diurnal, meaning they are most active during the day, unlike raccoons or other nocturnal pests. These sounds often indicate squirrels are nesting, foraging, or simply moving around within your structure. Ignoring these noises can allow the infestation to worsen, leading to more extensive damage over time. Prompt investigation by a professional can confirm the source of the noise and initiate appropriate removal procedures.

Chewed or Damaged Structures

Squirrels possess strong teeth that they constantly use for chewing and gnawing. This behavior helps keep their teeth at a manageable length and also serves as a way for them to gain access to food sources or build nests. Evidence of chewing on areas like wood trim, fascia boards, soffits, rafters, electrical wiring, and even plumbing pipes is a significant sign of squirrel activity. Damaged insulation in the attic is also a common finding. This chewing can compromise the structural integrity of your home and create dangerous situations, such as exposed electrical wires that pose a fire hazard. Addressing this damage and implementing exclusion techniques are crucial to preventing future issues.

Visible Entry Points

Look for openings around the exterior of your property where squirrels might be gaining access. These can be relatively small and might not be immediately obvious. Check for gaps or holes along the roofline, around chimneys, in vents, under eaves, or any areas where construction might not be perfectly sealed. Squirrels are adept at exploiting even minor vulnerabilities. Locating these entry points is paramount for our removal and prevention strategy. Sealing these openings is a primary focus of our exclusion services, ensuring that once the squirrels are out, they cannot easily return to your home.

Presence of Droppings or Urine

Squirrels, like any other animal, leave behind waste. Finding squirrel droppings in your attic, garage, or other areas of your home is a clear indication of an infestation. Squirrel droppings are typically small, pellet-shaped, and might be found in concentrated areas where they are nesting or frequently travel. The presence of urine can also create stains and emit a strong, unpleasant odor. Squirrel waste can contain pathogens and parasites, posing potential health risks to humans and pets. Proper cleanup and sanitization are essential after squirrel removal to ensure a healthy living environment. Our team includes thorough cleanup as part of our service.

Finding Nesting Materials

Squirrels build nests, often referred to as dreys, using materials like leaves, twigs, newspaper, and insulation. Discovering accumulations of such materials in your attic, wall cavities, or other enclosed spaces is a strong sign of squirrel nesting. These nests can be found in various locations within your home, providing shelter and a place to raise their young. The presence of a nest, especially with young squirrels, requires a sensitive approach to removal to ensure the safety of both the animals and the occupants of the home. Our technicians are trained to handle nesting situations humanely and effectively, including the removal of young when appropriate.

Witnessing Squirrels Entering or Exiting

Observing squirrels repeatedly entering or exiting your home at a specific point is a definitive sign of an active infestation. Pay attention to where you see them going in and out, especially around dusk and dawn when they are most active. This visual confirmation helps us pinpoint the primary access point, which is crucial for our removal efforts. Witnessing this behavior allows for a more targeted and efficient approach to trapping and exclusion. If you see squirrels regularly using a particular entry point, it's likely their primary access to your home, and sealing this point effectively is key to preventing future problems.

Unusual Odors

Squirrels, their nests, and their waste can produce distinct odors within your home. You might notice musky smells from urine, the odor of decaying organic matter from nesting materials, or even the smell of dead squirrels if any have perished within the structure. These odors can be unpleasant and can indicate an ongoing or past infestation. Identifying and addressing these odors is an important part of restoring a healthy living environment after squirrel removal. Our sanitization process helps eliminate these offensive smells and ensures your home smells fresh and clean again. Sometimes these odors can alert you to a problem even before you see visible signs of squirrels.

Damaged Bird Feeders or Gardens

While not always indicative of an indoor infestation, damaged bird feeders or gardens can suggest a high population of squirrels in your immediate vicinity. Squirrels are opportunistic feeders and will often target easy food sources. If you've noticed squirrels consistently raiding your bird feeders, digging up your bulbs, or otherwise damaging your landscaping, it increases the likelihood that they might eventually seek shelter inside your home, especially during colder weather. Managing the squirrel population in your yard can be a proactive step in preventing them from entering your home in the first place. While our primary focus is on indoor infestations, reducing attractants in your yard can complement our services.

Preventative Measures to Deter Squirrels

Once your squirrel issue has been resolved, taking proactive steps is crucial to prevent future infestations. Implementing preventative measures can save you time, money, and the stress of dealing with another squirrel problem. Here are some practical tips to make your property less attractive to squirrels:

Trim Overhanging Branches

Ensure that tree branches are trimmed away from your roof and siding. Squirrels are excellent climbers and can easily use branches as bridges to access your home. Maintaining a buffer zone around your house makes it more difficult for them to gain entry. We can advise on recommended distances during our inspection. This is a simple yet highly effective preventative measure. Regularly inspecting and trimming trees is an important part of property maintenance that can contribute to pest prevention.

Secure Potential Food Sources

Squirrels are attracted to readily available food sources. Secure your garbage cans with tight-fitting lids, avoid leaving pet food outdoors, and consider using squirrel-resistant bird feeders if you enjoy feeding birds. Cleaning up fallen birdseed or nuts promptly can also reduce attractants. Limiting access to food makes your property less inviting to foraging squirrels. Store pet food in airtight containers, whether it's indoors or outdoors. Even small amounts of spilled food can attract unwanted visitors.

Seal and Maintain Your Home's Exterior

Regularly inspect the exterior of your home for any potential entry points. Pay close attention to areas like the roofline, eaves, vents, and chimneys. Seal any gaps or openings with durable, squirrel-resistant materials. Repair damaged siding, soffits, or fascia boards promptly. Proper home maintenance is a significant factor in preventing wildlife intrusions. We can help you identify vulnerable areas during our inspection and provide recommendations for sealing them. A well-maintained home is less likely to become a target for squirrels seeking shelter.

Install Chimney Caps and Vent Covers

Uncapped chimneys and unprotected vents are common entry points for squirrels. Install sturdy chimney caps and vent covers to prevent squirrels from gaining access through these openings. These simple additions are highly effective in blocking potential entry routes. Ensure that the mesh on vent covers is small enough to prevent squirrels from squeezing through. These are often overlooked but are critical areas to secure. Our team can assess your chimney and vents and recommend appropriate protection.

Address Water Sources

While not as significant as food or shelter, readily available water sources can also attract wildlife. Fix any leaky pipes or faucets outdoors, and ensure that standing water is not accumulating on your property. While squirrels primarily get water from food, eliminating easy access to water can contribute to making your property less appealing. Ensure proper drainage around your home to prevent standing water. Even small puddles can be a water source for various pests.

Understanding Different Types of Squirrels in Maryland

Maryland is home to several squirrel species, each with slightly different behaviors and characteristics. While our squirrel removal techniques are effective for most nuisance squirrels, understanding the types you might encounter in Glen Burnie can be helpful. The most common culprits for indoor infestations are typically gray squirrels and flying squirrels.

Gray Squirrels

The Eastern Gray Squirrel (Sciurus carolinensis) is the most prevalent squirrel species in Maryland and is often the primary cause of indoor infestations. These squirrels are active during the day and are known for their agility and ability to climb. They are driven by the need to find food and shelter, especially as seasons change. Gray squirrels are adaptable and can thrive in urban and suburban environments, bringing them into close contact with human structures. They are known for burying nuts and seeds, which can also cause issues in gardens and yards. Their strong teeth allow them to chew through a variety of building materials to gain access to attics and other internal spaces. Understanding their typical behaviors like foraging and nesting helps us predict their movements and effectively set traps and seal entry points. They have two breeding seasons, one in late winter and another in summer, meaning there can be squirrel activity year-round. Their adaptability and intelligence make them persistent pests once they decide to inhabit a structure. We have extensive experience dealing with gray squirrels and their habits.

Flying Squirrels

Maryland is also home to both the Northern Flying Squirrel (Glaucomys sabrinus) and the Southern Flying Squirrel (Glaucomys volans). While less commonly encountered than gray squirrels, flying squirrels can also become a nuisance when they enter homes. These squirrels are nocturnal, meaning they are most active at night. If you hear scurrying sounds in your attic during the evening or early morning, it could be flying squirrels. They don't actually fly, but rather glide using a membrane between their legs and body. Flying squirrels often enter homes through small gaps high up on the structure. Because they are small and nocturnal, infestations can sometimes go unnoticed for longer periods. They tend to live in colonies, so if you have one flying squirrel, you likely have more. Dealing with flying squirrels requires understanding their nocturnal nature and specific entry points. Our expert technicians are equipped to handle flying squirrel infestations precisely and effectively, taking into account their unique behaviors. Their small size allows them to exploit smaller openings than gray squirrels, making thorough exclusion even more critical. Identifying the specific type of squirrel is an important part of developing the most effective removal plan.
